Aims & Scope

Oracle of Medicine and Medical Sciences (OMMS) is a quarterly, double-blind peer-reviewed, open-access, international journal for publishing innovative research in medicine and medical sciences across all medical and healthcare systems. OMMS publishes research of global significance enabling medical workers and other researchers to learn about developments and innovations both within and across these disciplines.

OMMS accepts Original Articles, Review Articles, Case Reports, Case Studies, Letters to the Editor, Short Communications, Method Articles, Editorials, Commentaries, and Book Reviews from all areas within medicine and medical sciences. All articles are checked for similarity to avoid plagiarism, reviewed quickly within the shortest possible time, and professionally copyedited, for quality and faster publication. OMMS publishes accepted articles on the internet immediately.

Article Submission

  1. OMMS accepts articles with APA and Vancouver Reference Styles.
  2. All tables and figures should appear at the appropriate places within the article, and not at the end of the article.
  3. Authors are strongly advised to download and look at previously published articles in OMMS.
  4. Before submission, please check your article again for common errors such as spelling errors, grammatical errors, etc.
  5. Satisfied with your article? Please send your article only as Microsoft Word (.doc or .docx) document, (font: Times New Roman; font size: 12) as an attachment to: editoromms@gmail.com or submit them through our online submission system.

Article Processing Charge (APC)

Fee: 100 USD (for all countries)

Payment should be made only after acceptance of the article for publication.

Editorial Policy

Oracle of Medicine and Medical Sciences (OMMS) abides with professional standards, such as:

  • Recommendations of the International Committee of Medical Journal Editors (ICMJE)
  • CSE’s White Paper on Promoting Integrity in Scientific Journal Publications
  • Core Practices of the Committee on Publication Ethics (COPE)
  • Policies of the World Association of Medical Editors (WAME)
